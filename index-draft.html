About

I am a sedimentologist and stratigrapher specializing in the geochemistry of ancient carbonate rocks, particularly those associated with mass extinction events. I teach at Amherst College, where I integrate fieldwork, laboratory analysis, and emerging technologies like machine learning and stable isotope geochemistry into upper-level geology courses such as GEOL-311.

Research

My work focuses on reconstructing paleoenvironmental change using sedimentary records, with particular emphasis on carbon and sulfur isotope stratigraphy, redox geochemistry, and volcanic proxies (e.g., mercury). Recent collaborations explore SPICE events, end-Triassic extinction signals, and Miocene climate dynamics.

Field regions include Mongolia, the Canadian Arctic, California, Namibia, and the Appalachians.

Teaching

I teach courses in sedimentology, stratigraphy, oceanography, and geobiology. GEOL-311, Sedimentology and Stratigraphy, includes project-based learning components using Jupyter notebooks, the SandAI image classifier, and stable isotope analysis of student-collected samples.
